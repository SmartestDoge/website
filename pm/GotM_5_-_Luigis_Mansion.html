Noah & Toad 13-Oct-18 11:23 AM
I like the novelty of Luigi's Mansion on the go, and the remembering the 3D effect is a really nice touch.
The inspiration of the PAL version of the hidden mansion was a nice addition, and boy, dooes it rev up the difficulty compared to normal.
Achievements and the boss attack mode add some more replayability, and it's fun to experiment with two players.
Overall, good dificulty rebalancing. Boo health goes down a lot quicker, meaning less room hopping, scripted ghosts spawn after ghosts have been defeated, the final boss seems a little less broken, and, oh my, Boolosus has been soundly nerfed in terms of greatly reduced speed and telepathic counter attacks.
Dropped Money also stays around a lot longer too, and cutscenes and animations freeze the dispawning time. You should be able to collect it all now, with no problem.
Key easter eggs still remain in tact, and there's some more text thrown into the game, too.
❌ The controls take a bit of getting used to. It's a learning experience, and some simple manuvers don't transfer to great. Oddly, mashing the control stick to drain boss hp is still in the remake, but the circle pad just dosen't seem like it can handle the power intake without the possible risk of getting broken, which is a huge shame for the boss rush mode. It will take a long time to match the percision of and ease of the the Gamecube controller. This is by far the biggest leg up the gamecube version has over the origional.
❌ Some hiccups in the framerate look a bit off. Both the gamecube and the 3DS go at 30 FPS, but there is something way more smoother about the gamecube version. It's not gamebreaking, but another strength of the gamecube.
Some glitches have been cut entirely. This is not all bad, but don't expect to be able to beat the game in 15 minnutes anymore.
Also not a fault, but there is still a perfectly good place for a door on the second floor of the foyer that would make a fantastic shortcut that would reduce backtracking. I still have no idea why it's not there like the stair case shortcut on the first floor. Oh well, the long way it is.

So, yeah. It's Luigi's Mansion on the go. It adds content if you want to see the game in a new light, but it's still Luigi's Mansion. For me, Luigi's Mansion in 3D and the PAL inspired Hidden mansion were justifyable for $40, but I see potential in a price drop for a returning fan or newcommer.

I don't give numberical ratings, but I'd say that I liked it, but still would recomend the Gamecube version to my closest friends to play first.

North Americans Beware! The Hidden Mansion you know has evolved into a beast, with many elements taken strait from the PAL version. As per usual, they hit twice as strong, and with the complete absence of hearts and poison mushrooms galore, you may want to invest in some amiibos.

If you are a huge Luigi's Mansion fan looking for a tough challenge, here's your game.(edited)
However, since Portrait Ghosts have more HP, there is a brand new tier of paintings, Platinum. For your standard portrait ghost, If you can score the big pearl after sucking continuous 140 HP, you get the prize. For bosses, you must finish with full health. (This can be easily exploited with reviving to full health with the Luigi amiibo before finishing off the boss) Almost all the Platinum cases hold the best painting designs yet, so get crackin'.
But with more pearls disrtibuted, how much more money is there in the new hidden mansion? Enough to surpass the A rank mansion and earn the highest S rank.
